What is happening in Kashmir?

Jammu and Kashmir is a region that shares borders with Pakistan, India, and China. This land is rich with natural resources, like fertile soil and freshwater, as it is located near the Karakoram and Himalayan mountain ranges. As the land enters through both India and Pakistan, these resources are vital both agriculturally and economically. However, who gains rights over the lands has and remains to be a topic fueled with dispute. After the partition of India and Pakistan in 1947, Kashmir was protected by Article 370 which granted Kashmir the right to have its own consistution. Both countries, Pakistan and India maintained control over two different parts of Kashmir. However, as of August 5, the Indian government scrapped Article 370, claiming the land for their own. Without the Article 370, Kashmir no longer has its own constitution, changing certain rights granted to Kashmiris.

Why is this important?

Ever since the scrapping of Article 370, Kashmir has been on a total lockdown by the Indian army. Citizens of Kashmir, predominantly Muslims, are being targeted, killed, raped, and trapped within their own homes. Internet, mobile services, landlines, and broadband connections have been shut down, restricting communication to and from Kashmir. Family members have not heard from each other. The safety of Kashmiris has been compromised, with over 600,000 Indian soldiers stationed within the area. The first ever mass blinding is happening in Kashmir, as innocents are being shot at with lead pellets which permanently damage the vision conning the term, "Dead Eyes". Over 50 days have passed since the Kashmir lockdown and tensions escalate between Pakistan and India, as Pakistan urges the United Nations to help in this breach of human rights. However, if justice is not brought soon, this could lead to an intense war in the East.
